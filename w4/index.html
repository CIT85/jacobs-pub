Physical Fitness

Inline skating engages various muscle groups such as the legs, core, and arms (more so for speed skaters) toning these muscles.

Inline skating is also an excelent cardio vascular workout helping to strengthen the heart, improve endurance, and burn calories.

Low Impact Exercise

Compared to other activities, inline skating is much more forgiving on the joints due to the gliding nature of the activity. Making this an ideal choice for individuals with jion issues that still want to get intense exercise.

Improved Balance and Coordination

Inline skating requires a great ability to balance and coordinate ones actions. Over time the demands inline skating has on these skills will help to improve them.

Versatile Recreation

There are many ways to inline skate

Perhaps more accurately there are multiple disciplines to inline skating

  1. Urban Skating

    The setup for urban skating is usuall the typical 4 wheel skates though 3 wheels can be common as well.

  2. Aggressive Skating

    Involes skating with smaller wheels and what is known as an anti-rocker setup and a H-block for primarily performing grinds.

  3. Speed Skating

    Usually the skates for this style of skating will have very large 3 wheeled setups allowing for improved speed at the cost of stability.

  4. Wizard Skating

    My personal favorite form of skating though I am too inexperienced to even attempt this yet.

    Wizard skating involves performing many different tricks without ever leaving the ground usually. This requires a great deal of experience but the results are truely amazing.

    Wizard skating setups commonly have a rocked setup where the front and rear wheels are raised slightly and 5 or more wheels are common for greatly increased stability.

More ways to skate

Aside from the above list skating can be enjoyed nearly anywhere there is pavement or smooth flate surfaces.

Such as:

  • Bike Trails
  • Indoor Rinks
  • Parks
  • Boardwalks

Social and Community Engagement

Inline skating has a very welcoming community willing to help new and experienced skaters to progress in the skill.
