Skating Gear

So you decided to try out skating



Here's What You'll Need
Equipment Importance Desription/Explanation
Helmet Very important For obvious reasons protect your head. Though honestly I have yet to hit mine but that is no reason to neglect a helmet.
Knee Pads Also important You actually want to learn to fall forwards and to land on your knees these stop that from hurting.
Wrist Guards Again important As a part of falling forward you will also land on your hands these also stop that from hurting.
Elbow Pads Not actually important Surprisingly I honestly don't think these do much I had a couple crazy falls and have yet to hit my elbows somehow. Obviousely not saying to not use them but they sure don't seem to be critical.
Skates Guess what you need these Surprise it is hard to skate without skates. Probably best to get something with lots of support ruling out dedicated speed skates at the very least.
And there we have it a table outlining skating equipment


Places to Learn
Location Assesment
Skatepark Surprisingly may not be the best place to start off at. This is because at least in the case of the one near me there is not a whole lot of room for a begginer to get around.
Parking lot Ah the good ole parking lot. Lots of space little in the way, you probably learned to drive here just be sure you are not around other cars.
Sidewalk Super smooth and long straight paths usually great place to get those basic strides in though can be a bit narrow at times
The main thing to look for in a place to learn is the smoother the better you'd be surprised at how much better a surface a sidewalk is vs a road with mild cracks.
